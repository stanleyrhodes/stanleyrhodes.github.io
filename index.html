Stan Rhodes

I investigate what big factors in environments, and in the social and cognitive properties of people, lead to patterns of organizational structure such as hierarchies and recorded policies. I'm currently a PhD student at Utah State University, advised by Dr. Jacob Freeman. I use agent-based models, network analysis, and statistical learning techniques.

My current research includes two big, different threads of inquiry.

The first focuses on using agent-based models to test the performance of hierarchical organizational structures. I use the model to generate varied problem environments which social agents then attempt to "solve" with limited decision-making ability. I'm looking for those conditions and dynamics that are necessary and sufficient for hierarchy to be worth it for the group of agents.

The second focuses on applying text mining (i.e. LDA topic modeling) to US law (i.e. the US Statutes at Large) to map the evolution of topics over time and jurisdiction.
